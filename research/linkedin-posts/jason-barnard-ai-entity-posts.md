# Jason Barnard - My Notes on Recent LinkedIn Posts

Profile: https://www.linkedin.com/in/jasonmbarnard/

I picked Jason Barnard because his angle is very different from generic AI content advice. He keeps focusing on how AI systems decide who to recommend, which makes his work feel more like recommendation engineering than traditional SEO commentary.

## Post 1

Title: Over the last year, AI systems have quietly become your most influential sales team

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasonmbarnard_ai-knowledgegraph-brandstrategy-activity-7428079166765436928-qJyj

Why I saved this:
This post feels highly relevant to my project because it reframes AI search as a recommendation system that can either mention a brand, hedge, or stay silent. That is a much more serious lens than just "how do I rank in AI."

What stood out to me:
- AI recommendations are shaped by whether a brand is clearly understood as an entity.
- Silence is expensive because it means the brand is not even entering the consideration set.
- Entity understanding compounds over time, so the brands that teach AI early may build a hard-to-catch advantage.

How this helps my future playbook:
This makes me want to include a section on "training the machine before scaling content." In other words, I should not only think about publishing more pages. I should think about whether AI systems can confidently explain who the brand is and why it should be recommended.

## Post 2

Title: What actually matters most for your entity home, the page itself, or schema markup?

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kalicube_entityseo-knowledgepanel-entityhome-activity-7425107363940069376-Rhru

Why I saved this:
I liked this because it gets specific. Instead of vague "optimize for AI" talk, it explains that schema is not magic by itself and that page structure plus clear semantics increase machine confidence.

What stood out to me:
- Schema is an enhancer, not the foundation.
- Strong page structure already teaches search engines and AI systems a lot.
- Confidence and trust are the real outputs, not just technical completeness.

How this helps my future playbook:
This gives me a useful bridge between AI visibility and content production. If I ever create content templates later, I want them to include entity clarity, structured context, and proof signals, not just keywords and headings.
