## Post 1

Title: How to work with AI visibility - findings from AI test

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/flow-agency_how-to-work-with-ai-visibility-findings-activity-7436712423350304768-fCbN

Why I saved this:
This is one of the strongest recent sources in my whole set for B2B SaaS. It summarizes a 5,000+ query study and makes a very important point: classic Google rankings and AI visibility overlap much less than people assume.

What stood out to me:
- Ranking well and showing up in AI answers are related, but not identical.
- Content diversification across the funnel matters more than relying on one page type.
- Video keeps appearing as a surprisingly strong format for AI-era visibility.

How this helps my future playbook:
This gives me a more advanced content production idea. Instead of only making blog posts, I should think about a multi-format system where pages, videos, and brand mentions work together.

## Post 2

Title: More buyers are turning to LLMs to research software, validate vendors, and make purchase decisions

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/flow-agency_more-buyers-are-turning-to-llms-to-research-activity-7401223526130536449-Wcya

Why I saved this:
I wanted another recent post that connects AI search directly to buying behavior, not just informational search. This matters a lot for my topic because I am more interested in useful commercial content systems than generic awareness content.

What stood out to me:
- LLMs are becoming part of the vendor research process before users even reach Google.
- Referral tracking, listicles, communities, and trust signals still influence what AI tools recommend.
- Traditional SEO is still relevant, but it now works inside a bigger discovery environment.

How this helps my future playbook:
This helps me think beyond "publish more articles." A stronger system would probably combine content, proof assets, off-site mentions, and commercial-intent pages that AI tools can cite when buyers compare vendors.
