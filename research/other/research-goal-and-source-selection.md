# Research Goal And Source Selection 

## Research Goal

My goal with this project is not only to collect expert content, but to build a source base strong enough to support a real playbook later for **AI-powered SEO content production**.

I wanted to study practitioners whose work could help answer questions like:

- how AI visibility differs from traditional rankings
- what kinds of content systems still matter in AI-search environments
- how brand, entities, citations, and cross-format content influence discoverability
- what practical workflows might actually work for B2B SaaS or similar contexts

## How I Chose My Sources

I did not want to rely on the first generic names that appeared in Google results.

Instead, I used:

- the Wellows article on top AI SEO agencies
- LinkedIn recommendations
- my discussion with Claude about strong SEO and AI practitioners
- extra research into expert backgrounds, their companies, and the kinds of content they publish

From that wider pool, I selected the experts whose material felt:

- more niche and interesting
- more practical than generic
- more likely to help me build a real framework or playbook later

## Why The Material Feels Strong Enough For A Future Playbook

This research set feels useful because it gives me a mix of:

- recent LinkedIn posts for fresh observations and framing
- YouTube interviews and talks for longer explanations
- transcript files for raw source material
- my own notes for what stood out and why it matters

That gives me a better foundation for later synthesis instead of only collecting links.
