## What I learned

- The overlap between classic top-10 rankings and AI Mode visibility was much lower than I would have guessed.
- AI systems inside Google seem to behave more probabilistically, which means visibility can be less stable even when a domain is trusted.
- Diversifying content across the funnel matters a lot more than relying on a single money page.
- Brand mentions on other trusted sites still seem to matter heavily for AI visibility.

## What surprised me

- The 25% overlap figure between top ranking URLs and AI Mode appearances was one of the most interesting findings.
- Video showing up as a strong format again lines up with what I already saw in the LinkedIn post notes.
- Helene's point that not all search systems are equal was a really useful reminder for how I should think about future optimization.

## Why this matters for my project

This gives me a stronger source than the LinkedIn post alone because I can now tie the short post summary to a longer spoken explanation. That makes the Helene source much more useful for my future playbook.

## Playbook ideas I want to keep

- Build topical clusters instead of chasing isolated keywords.
- Treat brand mentions and third-party visibility as core AI-search signals.
- Keep traditional Google SEO strong, but assume it does not automatically guarantee AI visibility.
- Use video as part of the content system, not as an afterthought.
