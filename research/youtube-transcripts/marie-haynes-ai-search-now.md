## My Notes

I picked Marie because she brings a much-needed quality and trust lens to this topic. She is not treating AI search like a shiny new traffic trick. She keeps returning to reputation, originality, usefulness, and how businesses can still matter when AI is summarizing so much of the web.

## What I learned

- Good SEO still matters, but AI search introduces new requirements around semantic richness, recommendation readiness, and reputation.
- Brands need to add something new to the web, not just repackage common knowledge.
- AI Mode changes the search experience from simple retrieval into something closer to an agentic research flow.
- Visibility is shifting away from "can I rank for this query?" toward "will the system trust and cite me for this topic?"

## What surprised me

- Her argument that Google used the web to organize information, and that the old model of monetizing generic informational content is ending, felt very blunt but also very convincing.
- I liked her test for content quality: if this content disappeared, would the web actually lose something?
- Her explanation of reputation and originality felt much more durable than trend-based AI advice.

## Why this matters for my project

This source is one of my best guardrails. It keeps my research from drifting into pure hype. If I build a playbook later, I want Marie's perspective to act like a filter: does this tactic actually create value and trust, or is it just trying to game visibility?

## Playbook ideas I want to keep

- Invest more in original insight and first-hand expertise.
- Treat reputation as a core input to AI visibility.
- Write content that is uniquely useful, not just keyword-aligned.
- Use AI-search tactics only when they still reinforce trust and quality.
