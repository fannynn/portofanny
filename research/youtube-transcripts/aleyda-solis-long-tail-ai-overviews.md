## My Notes

I picked this video because Aleyda is very good at turning broad industry changes into concrete strategic direction. Her focus on long-tail queries feels especially relevant now that AI Overviews and refined search journeys are changing which kinds of content still earn attention and clicks.

## What I learned

- Long-tail queries are becoming even more important because AI systems encourage users to refine and specify what they want.
- Broader informational searches are more exposed to AI-summary compression, while specific intent can still create strong opportunities.
- Search strategy should follow how people narrow their needs, not just how marketers group head terms.

## What surprised me

- I liked how clearly she framed long-tail focus as a response to AI behavior, not just as an old SEO best practice recycled again.
- The talk reinforced that specificity may become more valuable, not less, in an AI-mediated search experience.
- Her advice felt especially useful because it is immediately actionable without being simplistic.

## Why this matters for my project

This source helps me shape the editorial side of the playbook. It gives me a strong reason to favor sharper, more specific content targeting instead of broad generic pieces that AI can summarize away.

## Playbook ideas I want to keep

- Prioritize high-specificity content over generic awareness pieces.
- Map content to refined queries and follow-up questions.
- Use long-tail strategy as protection against AI-summary commoditization.
- Build content around nuanced intent, not only traffic size.
