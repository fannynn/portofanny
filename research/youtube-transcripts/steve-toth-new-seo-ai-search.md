## My Notes

I picked this video because it is one of the clearest bridges between AI search theory and commercial SEO execution. Steve keeps bringing everything back to buyer behavior, deal breakers, comparison content, and how the sales journey is getting compressed into one chat window.

## What I learned

- In AI search, one user can move from discovery to shortlist to decision inside a single conversation.
- Generic top-of-funnel content is much weaker now unless it is tied to a specific ICP or buying journey.
- Comparison pages, alternatives pages, and even three-way comparison pages are becoming much more important.
- Representation matters as much as discovery. The goal is not only to get found, but to be described correctly for the buyer's real criteria.

## What surprised me

- His framing around "deal breakers" was extremely useful. It is a better way to think about AI-era content than just keyword volume.
- He made a strong case that YouTube can be a low-lift but powerful way to influence long-tail AI visibility.
- I really liked the point that internal and off-site links help create maps of meaning, not just pass link equity.

## Why this matters for my project

This source is one of the most practical in my set. It helps me think about AI-powered SEO content production as a system for commercial intent, not just content scaling. It also gives me a much stronger angle for B2B SaaS research specifically.

## Playbook ideas I want to keep

- Build content around deal breakers, not just classic keyword buckets.
- Prioritize comparison and alternative frameworks earlier in the workflow.
- Create niche-specific content for ICPs instead of broad generic explainers.
- Use on-site pages, YouTube, LinkedIn, and third-party listicles together rather than treating the website as the whole game.
