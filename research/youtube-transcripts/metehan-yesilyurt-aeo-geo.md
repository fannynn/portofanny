## My Notes

I picked Metehan because his work feels experimental in a way that most SEO content does not. He talks about AI search as a behavior shift, but he also tests strange and creative ideas like memory hacking and "share on AI" buttons. That makes his work feel much more niche and original.

## What I learned

- Structured content matters a lot for AI retrieval, especially when entities and relationships are explicit.
- AI search visibility depends on more than rankings. It also depends on whether the model can understand the page quickly and confidently.
- Rich formatting like headings, bullets, tables, and clear entity connections can make content easier for LLMs to process.
- He treats AI search optimization as an ecosystem problem that includes brand mentions, citations, memory, and discoverability across platforms.

## What surprised me

- The "share on AI" / memory-hacking idea is one of the weirdest and most interesting concepts in my whole research set.
- He keeps emphasizing that being indexed and being understood are different things.
- His focus on semantic chunking and entity-rich writing feels much more advanced than generic advice about just adding FAQs or schema.

## Why this matters for my project

This source gives me some of the most unconventional thinking in the project. Even if some of the tactics end up changing fast, the mindset is valuable: design content for AI interaction patterns, not only for classic blue-link search.

## Playbook ideas I want to keep

- Test whether shorter, semantically clear sections help LLM understanding.
- Think about AI-native interaction patterns, not just search-engine rankings.
- Use entity clarity as part of the editorial process.
- Keep an eye on "memory" and repeat exposure, especially for branded discovery.
