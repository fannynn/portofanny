## My Notes

I picked Szymon because he feels like a strategist who still understands the deeper mechanics. He keeps talking about SEO as communication, audience understanding, and market context, not only technical checklists. That makes his perspective useful for the AI era, where classic ranking models are no longer the whole story.

## What I learned

- Search behavior is fragmenting across Google, YouTube, marketplaces, AI tools, and social platforms.
- SEO is becoming less about isolated ranking tricks and more about communication, relevance, and integrated traffic systems.
- Technical SEO still matters, but content structure, off-page visibility, and brand strategy matter more when search environments diversify.

## What surprised me

- I liked his point that future search work may become "less tooly and more about communication."
- He framed AI search and zero-click behavior as part of a larger shift in how users consume information, not as a one-off SEO update.
- He sounded very realistic about how much search platforms want to keep users inside their own ecosystems.

## Why this matters for my project

This source helps me zoom out. Some of my other sources are very tactical, but Szymon helps me think about the bigger strategic shift: AI-powered SEO content production now sits inside a broader discovery environment, not just inside Google rankings.

## Playbook ideas I want to keep

- Plan content for multiple discovery environments, not one channel only.
- Treat SEO as part of a broader communication and visibility system.
- Balance technical optimization with audience strategy and brand positioning.
- Think more about ecosystem presence and less about a single ranking metric.
