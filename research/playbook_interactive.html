B2B SaaS AI Visibility SEO Content Production Research-Driven

Core thesis

Stop optimizing only for ranking. Optimize for being selected, cited, and represented correctly.

What matters most

Deal breakers, entity clarity, comparison content, proof assets, and measurement that goes past clicks.

What dies first

Generic top-of-funnel filler, vague positioning, and fake confidence from messy AI reporting.

Operational steps
  • Map buyer deal breakers and follow-up questions before keyword expansion.
  • Fix entity clarity before scaling production.
  • Write pages that survive chunking and citation.
  • Prioritize comparison, alternatives, integration, and proof pages.
  • Build briefs from mined insights instead of blank prompts.
  • Track mentions, citations, and recommendation quality separately.
Memorable examples
  • Bad chunk: vague platform language that says nothing.
  • Good chunk: explicit audience, use case, integration, and exclusion.
  • Bad entity description: “leading all-in-one platform”.
  • Good entity description: category, buyer, strengths, and who it is not for.
Measurement logic
  • Mention rate: how often the brand appears.
  • Citation rate: how often the domain or page is cited.
  • Recommendation rate: how often the brand is framed as a fit.
  • Representation audit: whether the system describes the company correctly.
Why the playbook has a stronger point of view
  • It takes a side on AI visibility instead of hiding behind “it depends”.
  • It rejects schema-only thinking and generic filler production.
  • It names one expert to treat cautiously instead of pretending all ten are equally reliable.
