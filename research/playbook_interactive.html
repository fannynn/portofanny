Fanny's Playbook

This is the interactive web version of my playbook for AI visibility SEO in B2B SaaS content production. It is built to be used, not just read.

AI Visibility SEO B2B SaaS Content Production Research-Driven

Core Position

The useful shift is not “how do I rank one more page?” It is “how do I become easy for AI systems to understand, cite, and recommend when buyers ask specific questions?”

What matters now

Deal breakers, entity clarity, comparison content, proof assets, and representation quality.

What dies first

Generic top-of-funnel filler, vague positioning, and dashboards pretending noisy AI data is clean truth.

Main bias

This playbook is intentionally harsher on generic informational content than many SEO teams will be comfortable with.

3. Use the quick views

  • Build from deal breakers, not generic keyword buckets.
  • Fix entity clarity before scaling production.
  • Prioritize comparison, alternatives, integration, and proof pages.
  • Treat AI visibility as a current commercial problem, not a future curiosity.
  • Bad chunk: polished but empty platform language.
  • Good chunk: explicit audience, use case, integration, and exclusion.
  • Bad entity line: “leading all-in-one platform”.
  • Good entity line: category, buyer, strengths, and who it is not for.
  • Mention rate: how often the brand appears.
  • Citation rate: how often a page or domain is used as a source.
  • Recommendation rate: how often the brand is framed as a fit.
  • Representation audit: whether the system describes the company correctly.
  • Reject schema-only thinking.
  • Reject blind trust in AI reporting dashboards.
  • Reject the idea that AI visibility is just old SEO with a new label.
  • Follow experimental voices carefully, not blindly.

Useful Examples

Bad chunk

Our CRM platform empowers modern teams with flexible workflows, seamless collaboration, and innovative automation for growing businesses.

Too vague. It sounds polished but gives neither buyers nor machines anything concrete to work with.

Better chunk

This CRM is built for agencies with 10-100 staff that need QuickBooks sync, client-level permissions, and pipeline reporting without Salesforce-level setup time.

Specific audience, real use case, and clear selection criteria. That is much easier to retrieve and recommend.

Better comparison-page outline
  1. Direct verdict and best-fit summary
  2. Who each product is for
  3. Pricing differences
  4. Integration differences
  5. Onboarding friction
  6. Deal breakers
  7. Recommendation by scenario
